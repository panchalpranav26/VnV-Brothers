Why Life Insurance Matters

Life insurance helps your loved ones maintain financial stability in the event of your passing. It replaces lost income, covers debts, and funds essential goals such as education or retirement. Understanding your options ensures you make an informed decision aligned with your family's needs and financial plan.

Source: National Association of Insurance Commissioners (NAIC)

Types of Life Insurance

There are two primary categories of life insurance—each serves a unique purpose in a family’s financial strategy.

Type Purpose Duration Typical Use
Term Life Provides coverage for a set period (10–30 years) at an affordable premium. Temporary (expires after the term) Income replacement, debt coverage during working years.
Whole Life / Permanent Provides lifelong coverage and builds cash value that grows over time. Lifetime Estate planning, wealth transfer, or long-term financial security.

Source: Investopedia: Life Insurance Explained

Choosing the Right Policy

Selecting a policy depends on your financial goals, dependents, debts, and future plans. We recommend evaluating the following:

  • Your family’s income needs and current liabilities
  • Long-term goals such as education or home ownership
  • Balance between coverage affordability and duration
  • Presence of existing assets or savings

Frequently Asked Questions

Is life insurance necessary if I’m single?

Yes, especially if you have dependents, debt, or wish to leave a legacy. It can also cover final expenses or protect co-signers.

What happens if I stop paying my premiums?

For term policies, coverage lapses. For whole life, the policy may draw from cash value until exhausted.

Can I change my policy later?

Yes, you can convert or adjust coverage, but terms depend on your insurer and the policy type.
