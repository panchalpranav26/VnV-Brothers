Budgeting Basics

Welcome! At V & V Brothers LLC, our mission is to empower families with the knowledge and tools for financial literacy, protection, and wealth-building through healthy financial habits. This page will help you understand budgeting — what it is, why it matters, and how to get started.

Why Budgeting Matters

  • A budget provides a clear view of where your money is going — helping you make informed decisions. :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}
  • It helps you build savings, reduce stress around money, and remain prepared for unexpected events. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}
  • Research shows financial literacy, mindful budgeting and self-control strongly influence financial well-being. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

How to Build Your Budget — Step by Step

  1. Calculate your income: Determine your take-home pay (after taxes, retirement contributions, etc.). :contentReference[oaicite:4]{index=4}
  2. Track your spending: Record all your expenses for a period (fixed & variable) to understand where your money goes. :contentReference[oaicite:5]{index=5}
  3. Set realistic goals: Define short-term (3-12 months) and long-term goals (5+ years) and include them in your budget. :contentReference[oaicite:6]{index=6}
  4. Choose a budgeting system: Allocate income toward expenses, savings, and wants using a strategy that fits your life. :contentReference[oaicite:7]{index=7}
  5. Review & adjust: At the end of each month, compare actuals vs plan, note where you overspent, and adjust for next month. :contentReference[oaicite:8]{index=8}

Popular Budgeting Methods

50-30-20 Rule

This method suggests: 50% of income for needs, 30% for wants, and 20% for savings or debt repayment. :contentReference[oaicite:9]{index=9}

Zero-Based Budgeting

Every dollar of your income is assigned a job — income minus expenses equals zero. You decide where each dollar goes ahead of time. :contentReference[oaicite:10]{index=10}

Envelope (Cash-Stuffing) System

Using physical (or virtual) “envelopes” for each spending category helps you limit overspending and stay on track. :contentReference[oaicite:11]{index=11}
