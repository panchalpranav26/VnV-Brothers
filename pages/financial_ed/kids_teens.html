Kids & Teens: Building Smart Money Habits

Empowering the next generation with confidence, curiosity, and skills to make wise money choices.

Source references: FDIC Money Smart, CFPB Youth Financial Education, FINRA Foundation

For Kids (Ages 6–12)

Kids learn best through play and examples. Introducing money basics at a young age builds a foundation for lifelong financial wellness.

Concept Activity Goal
Saving & Sharing Use three jars labeled Save, Spend, and Share. Teaches purpose-based saving and generosity.
Needs vs. Wants Play a “money choice” game using pictures of toys vs. essentials. Helps kids recognize priorities and trade-offs.
Goal Setting Have kids draw a picture of something they want to save for. Encourages patience and delayed gratification.

For Teens (Ages 13–18)

Teens begin handling real money decisions — earning from part-time jobs, using debit cards, or planning for college. Early financial literacy helps them manage credit, spending, and savings responsibly.

Topic What to Learn Skill Outcome
Budgeting Basics Create a 50/30/20 budget plan (needs/wants/savings). Develops awareness of spending categories.
Understanding Credit Use credit-score simulators (like MyCreditUnion.gov). Builds understanding of borrowing responsibly.
Banking Skills Learn how checking/savings accounts work and use online banking demos. Promotes real-world money management confidence.
