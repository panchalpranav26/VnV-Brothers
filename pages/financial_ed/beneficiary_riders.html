Beneficiaries and Riders

Understanding how beneficiaries and policy riders work is essential to protecting your family’s financial future. These elements ensure your life insurance coverage aligns with your values and changing life needs.

Beneficiaries: Choosing Who You Protect

A beneficiary is the person or entity that receives your life insurance proceeds. It’s a key decision — one that defines your legacy and ensures your loved ones are supported when they need it most.

Beneficiary TypeDescriptionExample
Primary BeneficiaryThe first person or organization to receive policy proceeds upon your passing.Your spouse, child, or trust.
Contingent BeneficiaryReceives benefits only if the primary beneficiary has passed away or cannot claim them.Your sibling, parent, or charity.
RevocableYou can change this beneficiary anytime during the policy’s life.Updating your child’s name after marriage or adoption.
IrrevocableOnce named, this beneficiary cannot be changed without their written consent.Often used in divorce settlements or business agreements.

Source: California Department of Insurance

Riders: Tailoring Protection to Your Life

Riders are add-ons to your life insurance policy that extend or customize coverage. They offer flexibility for changing needs, such as income replacement, disability, or child coverage.

Allows early access to part of the death benefit if you are diagnosed with a terminal illness.

Waives future premium payments if you become totally disabled and unable to work.

Provides term life coverage for your children, often convertible to permanent coverage later.

Allows you to increase coverage at specific times without another medical exam.
